Some curious facts about Rexburg, Idaho:
-Rexburg is known as the "Gateway to Yellowstone" due to its proximity to the famous Yellowstone National Park. It is only about an hour and a half drive from Rexburg to the west entrance of the park.
-Brigham Young University-Idaho, one of the largest private universities in the state, is located in Rexburg. The university is affiliated with The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and has a student population of over 20,000.
-The Rexburg Tornado of 2008 was one of the most devastating natural disasters in the city's history. The tornado, which was rated an EF2, destroyed many buildings and homes in the city and caused millions of dollars in damage.
-Rexburg is known for its large population of young families. In fact, the median age of residents in Rexburg is just 22 years old, making it one of the youngest cities in the United States.
-The city is home to the Legacy Flight Museum, which houses a collection of historic military aircraft, including planes from World War II and the Vietnam War. The museum also hosts airshows and other events throughout the year.
